Mountain Pose (Tadasana): Stand tall with feet together, arms by your sides. Engage thighs, lift chest, and reach arms overhead. Benefits: Improves posture, strengthens thighs and ankles. Downward Facing Dog (Adho Mukha Svanasana): Start on hands and knees, lift hips towards the ceiling, forming an inverted V-shape. Keep hands shoulder-width apart, and feet hip-width apart. Benefits: Stretches the whole body, strengthens arms and legs, calms the mind. Warrior I (Virabhadrasana I): Step one foot back, keeping front knee bent and arms raised overhead. Hips face forward. Benefits: Strengthens legs, opens hips, and chest. Warrior II (Virabhadrasana II): Similar to Warrior I, but hips and shoulders open to the side. Front knee remains bent, arms extend parallel to the floor. Benefits: Strengthens and stretches legs, improves focus. Tree Pose (Vrikshasana): Stand on one leg, place the sole of the other foot on the inner thigh or calf. Balance while hands are in prayer position or raised overhead. Benefits: Improves balance, strengthens legs, and enhances concentration.
